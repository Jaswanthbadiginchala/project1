Mission Objectives

The Chandrayaan-4 mission aims to land a rover near the Moon's south pole to study water ice and mineral composition.

Launch Details

The spacecraft lifted off from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre at 6:00 AM IST, marking a major milestone for India's space program.

Key Features of the Mission

The mission includes a new-generation lander, a high-resolution camera system, and AI-assisted rover navigation.

International Collaborations

ISRO has partnered with agencies like NASA and ESA to share data and coordinate lunar mapping efforts.
